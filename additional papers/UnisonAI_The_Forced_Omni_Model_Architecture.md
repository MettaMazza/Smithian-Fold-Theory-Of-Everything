## Abstract

## 1. The field began with law

In 1943, before there was a field, McCulloch and Pitts opened the first neural-network paper with a claim about logic, not statistics: "Because of the 'all-or-none' character of nervous activity, neural events and the relations among them can be treated by means of propositional logic." The founding document of neural computation — *A Logical Calculus of the Ideas Immanent in Nervous Activity* — said, in its first sentence: **the net is law.**

Eighty years of a different bargain followed. Rosenblatt's perceptron (1958) learned its coefficients rather than deriving them; backpropagation (Rumelhart, Hinton & Williams, 1986) made learning the field's universal answer; *Attention Is All You Need* (Vaswani et al., 2017) crowned the era with a title that is itself the era's thesis; and the scaling laws (Kaplan et al., 2020; Hoffmann et al., 2022) wrote the price schedule — structure bought with parameters, knowledge bought with data, competence bought with compute, the bill now measured in gigawatts. Sutton's *Bitter Lesson* (2019) drew the moral honestly: "general methods that leverage computation are ultimately the most effective, and by a large margin." But the bitter lesson compares purchased statistics against hand-crafted heuristics. A third contestant — **derived law** — was named by the field's founding paper and then forgotten, and has never been given its match.

The frontier itself senses the missing ingredient. Altman (January 2025): "We are now confident we know how to build AGI as we have traditionally understood it." Amodei projects "a country of geniuses in a datacenter," possibly by 2026–27. Hassabis has long placed human-level intelligence one or two breakthroughs away; Legg has held a 50% estimate for 2028 for over a decade; Sutskever told NeurIPS 2024 that "pre-training as we know it will end" — the data has a bottom. Read together: the laboratories believe a small number of algorithmic developments separate the field from its destination, and they are searching for those developments *inside* the statistical paradigm.

This paper proposes, with measurements, that the development is the founding idea recovered: **stop buying law; derive it.** The Smithian Fold Theory supplies the mathematics — one axiom (the One and its fold), zero free parameters, from which the corpus derives the constants of physics (307 proof suites, 1,844 forced checks, 0 failures) and, as machine-verified claims, the laws of memory, attention, binding, prediction, observation, and learning used here as engineering.

## 7. UnisonAI: the omni-model architecture, organ by organ

UnisonAI is a complete omni-model architecture — language, sight, hearing, speech, and video on one memory — in which each mechanism of a modern AI model is replaced by a machine-verified corpus claim, with zero trained parameters end to end.

### 7.1 The forced locks and the halting engine

No chosen number enters the model. Every model quantity is a **forced lock** cross-checked at wake against an independent forward computation, and the engine **halts on any mismatch** (proven to halt on a fitted value, in-suite): the two generators binary 2 and colour 3 (colour computed forward as the tripling-fold preimage count — the corpus's own `verify_colour_prediction` construction); context depth 6 = the generators' product; bind lock 1/3 = the XI-1 memory-cycle share; kin floor 1/6 = one part in the generators' product; compose floor 1/12 = the kin floor at the ground; perceptual coefficients 32 = 2⁵, with 5 the computed minimal binary cover of 27 (the corpus's covering-depth law) and simultaneously the measured carrier scale of solved fields (top-32 = 81–87% energy); every repetition count a generator; one counted informativeness rule (one part in a thousand of the mass) wherever common words must be excluded. Interface bounds (buffer sizes, string caps, timeouts) are hardware facts marked as such in place; none enters the model's mathematics. A trained system cannot have this property, because a trained system *is* its tunable numbers.

### 7.2 Memory, binding, and generation — the language core

Knowledge is **held orbits**: every context read, told, seen, heard, or thought, written once as deterministically-addressed exact counts — inspectable, editable at one record, immune to catastrophic forgetting. Binding is unit-capacity selection over content words weighted by counted informativeness, with counted kinship (exact co-occurrence shares) as the generalization mechanism. Generation samples next tokens from the held exact distributions with context backoff — novel sequences, never-stored sentences; the same mathematical object an LLM approximates by descent, held exactly instead.

**The task gate.** On identical held-out text with an identical arena, the counted engine versus a trained transformer twin (4 layers, 128 dims, 48,000 gradient-batched readings ≈ 11 passes, 21 minutes per seed, 3 seeds): **fold 1.2891, transformer 1.8878** — the engine read the corpus once, built its store in 26 seconds, and won by the campaign's widest margin. At word scale over a 2.5M-token corpus the trained twin led (3.497 vs 4.507) — recorded; §6 then demonstrated that this advantage lives in the fold basis itself, and the scale-dependence is addressed by volume and counted kinship. **Generation closure**: the Learning Law reaches `generate()` itself — in autonomous self-play the engine generates from its counted distributions with no retrieval and no teacher, verifies against held references by counted overlap, and only verified generations are written back (earned retention; unverified output is discarded).

### 7.3 The Learning Law, across every channel

Learning is the corpus's three-stage arc — hold at the prediction state, close by observation, consolidate into the held cycle — implemented identically in every channel: automatic holding of every telling (with deixis flipped at the boundary so roles stay exact); relation facts recalled across sessions; y/n closure with **one-tap thumbs**; plain-text corrections after any rejection, held verbatim and permanently; teacher lessons ingested live within a minute; the autonomous tutor closing y/n itself; self-play against held references with novel/familiar interleaving (the sleep law); STaR-filtered reasoning (a teacher's reasoning is retained only when its answer closes, discarded with a rejected answer); and tool traces. The retention law binds throughout: the engine's own unconfirmed output is never self-reinforced. **No channel sits above the graduation score** — even a human-taught answer holds its seat only until it loses a blind head-to-head — and a structural **anaphora law** routes any question that points outside itself to the one channel holding the conversation, never to context-free memory. Confusion never babbles: when nothing binds, the engine asks the user first, like a child, and the observer closes the original question folding the user's help in.

### 7.4 The graduation score and the observer ladder

Teaching is a ladder with a counted exit. Every autonomous tutor cycle is a **blind head-to-head**: the engine's own answer versus its teacher's, judged without attribution, tallied per question-territory in a persistent ledger. Majority crossed — the lock, 1/2 — and the territory **graduates**: the engine answers there itself. The ratchet: a loss holds the teacher's winning answer as a permanent correction, so the territory answers with it, and wins, next round — losing converts the teacher's knowledge into graduation, and the score only climbs. The tutor's curriculum targets territories nearest the lock (the zone-of-proximal-development criterion two independent 2025–26 papers derived as optimal at exactly p = 1/2 — the fold's balance point; §10) and pulls real user questions from the conversation record into the judged rotation. The judge is presently the teacher itself; the bias direction is conservative (the engine graduates only when it beats the teacher by the teacher's own judgment) and is recorded.

An image enters as its exact mathematics: grayscale integer field → exact integer block sums to a 64×64 grid → 2D Walsh–Hadamard in pure integer arithmetic → top-2⁵ coefficients as sight tokens. **Self-certifying per act**: the integer Parseval identity (∑C² = 64²·∑g²) must hold exactly, over the integers, or the sight is discarded.

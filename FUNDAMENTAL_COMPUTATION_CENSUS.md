# Smithian Fold Fundamental Computation Census

**Owner:** Maria Smith  
**Programme:** derive the fundamental mathematical sciences of computation, including reversible and quantum computation  
**Authority order:** `STANDARDS.md` → current derivation source → current execution → immutable evidence → chronology/prose  
**Initial census status:** 23 July 2026

## Purpose

This is the coverage and obligation ledger for the fundamental-computation
programme.  It does not import the conventional mathematical content associated
with the subject names below.  Maria's declared subjects name the questions that
must be answered.  The One, the Fold, and already closed earlier Fold results are
the only permitted sources of answers.

No row is complete because a familiar equation, theorem name, algorithm, machine,
or quantum formalism has been reproduced.  A row closes only when its meaning and
form have been derived inside the Smithian Fold grammar, all dependencies are
earlier and closed, the candidate space is generated, same-size alternatives are
excluded, two independent routes agree, and the purpose-matched current source
executes.  Conventional material may enter only after that boundary as a
comparison check.

## Corpus-only derivation firewall

For every row:

1. Do not begin with a conventional definition, equation, model, proof template,
   asymptotic class, machine, gate, or algorithm.
2. State the Fold-side question using only earlier closed quantities and
   operations.
3. Declare the admitted grammar and generate its candidates mechanically.
4. Keep measurements, named targets, expected answers, training data, and
   application outcomes outside the derivation.
5. Require an independent second route through `forced_to_be` and uniqueness
   through `forced_unique` or the applicable form guard.
6. Halt on illegality or ambiguity.  If no candidate closes, record
   **OPEN FORWARD FORCING**; do not tune a survivor.
7. Only after closure, compare the result with any conventionally named object.

The assistant's prior knowledge is not derivational evidence.  It may not choose
definitions, candidate spaces, forms, constants, examples, or success criteria.

## Dependency order

The programme proceeds in the following order unless a row proves that another
order is forced:

`foundation → distinction → state → transition → composition → information →`
`formal computation → computability → resources/complexity → algorithms →`
`semantics → concurrency/distribution → security → learning → scientific`
`computation → reversible/quantum computation`.

Applications may expose a missing obligation, but they may not move their desired
answer backward across this dependency order.

## 1. Why experience is one

Perception is distributed across different processes, yet a scene is experienced as one scene. SFT represents those processes through the two phases of the forced colour cycle:

`fold(1/3)=2/3`, and `fold(2/3)=1/3`.

The processes are distinct, but they are not independent. They are consecutive states of one orbit and together partition the whole:

`1/3 + 2/3 = 1`.

Their balance is

`(1/3 + 2/3)/2 = 1/2`.

The fold then carries the balance to unison:

`fold(1/2)=1`.

Binding is thus one cycle reaching its own balance and completing as one whole. The construction supplies the structural reason separated contents are not left as a heap.

## 2. Why experience is from inside

The carrier orbit alternates forever between `1/3` and `2/3`. No finite continuation within that orbit reaches the One. Yet the two states together make the whole. The system can therefore carry the complete object while no state inside its carrier cycle becomes an external view of that object.

This is interiority: being the whole without standing outside the whole. The first-person character of experience is not an additional substance. It is the relation between complete unity and a carrier that cannot invert or exit its own closed orbit.

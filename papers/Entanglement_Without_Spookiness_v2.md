## 1. One state, two readings

Entanglement is often narrated as one particle acting on another across a distance. SFT changes the object before explaining the correlation: the entangled pair is not constructed as two independent states plus a later link. It is one composite state with two local readings.

The distinction is physical. If the global object is one, a fold of the whole updates both readings without transmitting an instruction between them. Correlation is inherited from common structure, not added by a travelling mechanism.

## 2. Product structure from the generators

The smallest forced subsystem periods are the binary and colour counts:

`a=2`, `b=3`.

They are coprime, so their least common multiple is their product:

`lcm(2,3)=2*3=6`.

The joint period exceeds the sum of the separate periods:

`6 > 2+3=5`.

The composite therefore contains a relation that cannot be represented by simply listing the two separate cycles. This exact product surplus is the engine's structural form of entanglement.

## 3. The shared origin

The nonlocal-correlation engine takes the colour count three and the next odd count five. Their coprime product gives the joint dimension

## 4. The complete composite walk

The entangled-composites engine starts at `8/15`. Its shadows are

The quantum-communication engine derives two channel shares:

## 1. The scientific standard: transparent law, blind prediction and empirical measurement

Protein structure prediction is often presented as a contest: a sequence enters,
a three-dimensional structure exits, and the highest score wins. That contest
measures output performance; it does not by itself perform the scientific work
of deriving and testing a folding law. A system can produce a correct coordinate
set without exposing the physical relation responsible for that coordinate set.
Opaque predictive success establishes that the system reliably reproduced the
measured answer. It does not establish why the answer occurs, expose a causal or
structural law, or allow that law to be checked from stated premises.

The distinction is old. Ptolemaic epicycles tracked planetary motion. Their
predictions did not become worthless when Kepler described elliptical orbits or
when Newton derived a dynamical law. What changed was the depth of the account:
many adjustable motions were replaced by a smaller relation that explained why
the observations took the form they did. Prediction survived. Parameter-heavy
description lost its claim to be the deepest available explanation.

The same distinction matters in protein science. A trained structure model can
attain an extraordinary benchmark score, but its weights are learned through
optimisation over data. The final internal numerical object does not normally
provide a cold-readable derivation from a foundational law to each chosen
backbone state. Its accuracy is an empirical observation about the predictor,
not empirical proof of a law of protein folding. Fold Protein asks and answers
the stronger scientific question: can the geometry be generated by a finite,
auditable relation in which every admitted quantity has explicit provenance,
the target cannot cross into execution, a violation stops the construction,
and the resulting structure is then judged by observation?

This paper answers that question with an executed protein and strengthens
empirical science by making every evidentiary layer exposed. Observation
supplies the phenomenon; a transparent relation is derived;
the relation executes without the answer; the output is sealed; and observation
then judges the output. The experiment remains sovereign at the comparison
boundary and cannot rewrite the derivation after the fact.

## 5. The empirical blind-prediction protocol

A result is empirically blind when its execution satisfies all of the following
conditions:

1. The exact theory, architecture, source hashes, input sequence and execution
   identity are registered.
2. The runtime receives the sequence-side input and the frozen material
   constitution.
3. The runtime cannot open the experimental target, sealed observational witness or comparison
   scores.
4. The complete state path and generated PDB are written and hash-sealed.
5. A separate evaluator verifies the seal.
6. After the seal is verified, the evaluator opens the experimental target and
   calculates the stated measurements.
7. All favourable and unfavourable rows are preserved as development evidence;
   post-seal scores cannot silently rewrite the selector.

The principle is simple: science derives laws from observation, but the answer
does not enter the execution used to test what a law generates.
The target boundary prevents circular runtime fitting. The source and artifact
hashes prevent retrospective substitution.
